Question:

What is the area of this figure?

Write your answer using decimals. Use 3.14 for \(\pi\).
___ square kilometres

Figure Visualization

Figure Description:

The image shows a compound figure composed of two rectangles and a quarter circle. The left rectangle has a height of 10 kilometres and a width of 6 kilometres. Attached to its right is a second rectangle, which is 25 kilometres tall and 8 kilometres wide. On the right side of the second rectangle, a quarter circle is attached with a radius of 11 kilometres.

Solution Steps:

  1. Step 1/9: Divide the figure into separate shapes: Start with rectangle A. Rectangle A is 6 kilometres wide and 10 kilometres tall.
  2. Step 2/9: Multiply: 6 \(\times\) 10 = 60 The area of rectangle A is 60 square kilometres.
  3. Step 3/9: Look at rectangle B. Rectangle B is 8 kilometres wide and 25 kilometres tall.
  4. Step 4/9: Multiply: 8 \(\times\) 25 = 200 The area of rectangle B is 200 square kilometres.
  5. Step 5/9: Look at quarter circle C. Quarter circle C has a radius of 11 kilometres.
  6. Step 6/9: Solve for the area of quarter circle C.
  7. Step 7/9: \(\frac{3.14 \cdot 11^2}{4} = 94.985$ The area of quarter circle C is 94.985 square kilometres.
  8. Step 8/9: Now add the areas of all the shapes. 60 + 200 + 94.985 = 354.985
  9. Step 9/9: The area is 354.985 square kilometres.
