Write an equation that says that the length of the green line is equal to the length of the black line.
Visual representation for Gr5_34_4_1

A horizontal line segment diagram. The top line (green) is labeled '8'. The bottom line (black) is divided into two parts: the left part is labeled 'q' and the right part is labeled '3'. Both lines have the same total length.

Solution:

The green line has length 8. The black line is made of two parts: q and 3. So, the equation is 8 = q + 3. We can also write this as q + 3 = 8, or 8 = 3 + q, or 3 + q = 8. To solve for q: q = 8 - 3 = 5.
