Question:

What is the area of this figure?

Write your answer using decimals. Use 3.14 for \(\pi\).
___ square kilometres

Figure Visualization

Figure Description:

The image shows a compound figure composed of two rectangles and a quarter circle. The left rectangle has a height of 11 kilometres and a width of 6 kilometres. Attached to its right is a second rectangle, which is 22 kilometres tall and 4 kilometres wide. On the right side of the second rectangle, a quarter circle is attached with a radius of 12 kilometres.

Solution Steps:

  1. Step 1/9: Divide the figure into separate shapes: Start with rectangle A. Rectangle A is 6 kilometres wide and 11 kilometres tall.
  2. Step 2/9: Multiply: 6 \(\times\) 11 = 66 The area of rectangle A is 66 square kilometres.
  3. Step 3/9: Look at rectangle B. Rectangle B is 4 kilometres wide and 22 kilometres tall.
  4. Step 4/9: Multiply: 4 \(\times\) 22 = 88 The area of rectangle B is 88 square kilometres.
  5. Step 5/9: Look at quarter circle C. Quarter circle C has a radius of 12 kilometres.
  6. Step 6/9: Solve for the area of quarter circle C.
  7. Step 7/9: \(\frac{3.14 \cdot 12^2}{4} = 113.04$ The area of quarter circle C is 113.04 square kilometres.
  8. Step 8/9: Now add the areas of all the shapes. 66 + 88 + 113.04 = 267.04
  9. Step 9/9: The area is 267.04 square kilometres.
