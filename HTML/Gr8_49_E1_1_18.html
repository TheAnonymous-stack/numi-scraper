What is the surface area of this cylinder? Use $\pi \approx 3.14$ and round your answer to the nearest hundredth. ___ square centimetres

Solution Steps

1 Step 1: Find the radius and height of the cylinder. radius=3 height=19
2 Step 2: Find the area of the circle on the top of the cylinder. Use 3.14 for $\pi$. $A_{top} = \pi r^2$ $\approx 3.14 \times 3 \times 3$ $\approx 28.26$
3 Step 3: The circle on the bottom of the cylinder is the same, so: $A_{bottom} = A_{top} \approx 28.26$
4 Step 4: Find the circumference of the top circle. $C = 2\pi r$ $\approx 2 \times 3.14 \times 3$ $\approx 18.84$
5 Step 5: Now find the area of the curved surface. The curved surface is a rectangle. One side length is the height, and the other side length is the circumference of the circle. $A_{side} = C \times h$ $\approx 18.84 \times 19$ $\approx 357.96$
6 Step 6: Now add the areas to find the surface area of the cylinder. $\text{Surface area} = A_{top} + A_{bottom} + A_{side}$ $\approx 28.26 + 28.26 + 357.96$ $\approx 414.48$
7 Step 7: The surface area of the cylinder is about 414.48 square centimetres.
Answer: 414.48 square centimetres
