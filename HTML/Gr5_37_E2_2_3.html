Complete the relative frequency table for the data.

Solution:

Step 1/5: To find the relative frequency of each marble, first find the total number of marbles Jake found.

Step 2/5: 33+1+6=40 Jake found 40 marbles. Write the relative frequency of each type as a fraction with a denominator of 40.

Step 3/5: The frequency for Red is $\frac{33}{40}$. The frequency for Blue is $\frac{1}{40}$. The frequency for Green is $\frac{6}{40}$.

Step 4/5: Now write each relative frequency as a percent. Since a percent tells you a part out of 100, write an equivalent fraction with a denominator of 100. $\frac{33}{40} = \frac{33\times2}{40\times2} = \frac{83}{100}$ The relative frequency of Red is 83%. You can write the other relative frequencies as percents the same way.

Step 5/5: The relative frequency of Blue: 2%. The relative frequency of Green: 15%.
