Question 1_46 | Grade 8 | Tag: Gr8_50_E1
What is the length of the hypotenuse?
$c$ = ___ kilometres

Given Information

Right triangle with legs:

a = 52 km

b = 336 km

Find: hypotenuse c

Formula

Pythagorean Theorem:

$a^2 + b^2 = c^2$

Answer

The length of the hypotenuse:

c = 340 kilometres

Solution Steps

1 Use the Pythagorean theorem, with $a=52$ and $b=336$.
2 $a^2+b^2=c^2$ Pythagorean theorem
3 $52^2+336^2=c^2$ Plug in $a=52$ and $b=336$
4 $2704+112896=c^2$
5 $115600=c^2$
6 $\sqrt{115600}=\sqrt{c^2}$
Take the square root on both sides
$340=c$
7 The length of the hypotenuse is 340 kilometres.
